WHAT ARE MANAGED PAYROLL SERVICES?

Rather than having your own payroll department, you can outsource your payroll responsibilities to a company that specialises in payroll services.

The third-party provider will manage all aspects of your payroll process; however, in order for them to do so, you must provide them with information about your employees, such as the hours worked and the rate of pay for each pay period.

It is up to you how much of the payroll process you outsource to a third party; completely outsourcing the process will include many of the functions, such as calculating gross wages, setting up a payroll system, and making any necessary national insurance deductions.

It will also ensure that you are in compliance with changing tax and payroll regulations.

They will be able to print and email payslips, as well as print and send them.

They can deposit funds into your employees' bank accounts, enter any and all payroll data, prepare and submit tax filings for your employees, and complete and file any other necessary paperwork not listed, including submissions to HMRC.

ARE THERE ANY BENEFITS TO MANAGED PAYROLL SERVICES?

The advantages of outsourcing your payroll to a third party to manage your payroll services are numerous.

You will immediately notice a financial benefit; payroll companies can often save you money because you no longer need to hire someone, or a team of people, to perform the job.

There are additional savings to be made, such as any licencing fees for payroll software, any supplies and equipment, such as perforation machines, ink and paper, printers, and you will not have to pay for ongoing payroll training and development.

When you consider outsourcing your payroll to a third-party payroll provider, you will immediately see time and money savings for you and your company. This relieves your employees of the complex administrative duties associated with payroll, allowing them to concentrate on the actual business.

There will be additional benefits in terms of increased HMRC compliance and security because you will be outsourcing to an HMRC compliant payroll provider.

Furthermore, because the payroll provider will be managing multiple accounts from various companies, they will ensure that they have cutting-edge encryption, firewalls, and other security devices to protect your and their clients' employees' data.

This reduces the risk of theft, security breaches, or fraud while also keeping any data confidential and secure.

Even if you hire a third party to process your company's payroll, you will still have oversight and control over the process.

You will have complete access and control over the process through reports, payroll software, and, most importantly, access to your payroll manager who can answer any of your questions or deal with any of your employees' questions.

SHOULD MY BUSINESS INVEST IN MANAGED PAYROLL SERVICES?

Managed payroll services can benefit any type or size of business.

If your company is large and already has its own in-house payroll department, you will immediately see a savings because you will no longer have to pay wages or benefits to a dedicated payroll team because this will be outsourced at a much lower rate.

If you are a medium or small business with 20 or fewer employees, you will also benefit from outsourcing your payroll to a third party.

You will gain real value if you are a business owner who handles every aspect of your company's operation.

To be in compliance with payroll and tax regulations, you must have extensive experience and knowledge to ensure that no mistakes are made, as mistakes can be costly.

If you lack experience or specialised knowledge, you are much more likely to make payroll mistakes, which can result in large penalties and costly fines when dealing with HMRC.

When you decide to outsource your payroll services, you will gain the expertise and dedication of payroll specialists.

Furthermore, and most importantly, you save time, as the payroll process can be complex, lengthy, and time consuming.

This will allow you to concentrate more on the core of your organisation and the expansion of your business.
